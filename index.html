About Me

Hi, I am Biswajit Bera, a PhD Scholar in the Department of Mathematics and Computing at IIT (ISM) Dhanbad, working under the supervision of Dr. Sudhakar Kumawat. My research focuses on Computer Vision, Computational Photography, and Domain Generalization, particularly in developing robust AI models for drone-based aerial imagery.

I completed my M.Sc. (CGPA: 8.74/10.0) and B.Sc. (CGPA: 9.13/10.0) in Mathematics & Computing. I have worked as a Junior Research Fellow on AI models for drone imagery and won the Champion title at the CLASH of T-AI-TANS Hackathon among 300+ teams.

My research interests include deep learning for vision systems, semi-supervised learning, aerial imagery analysis, and multi-domain adaptation. I'm passionate about developing generalizable AI solutions and am always open to research collaborations in Computer Vision and AI.

Research Projects

  • Geometry-Preserving Contrastive Learning for Aerial Object Detection (Submitted to ICPR 2026, Jan 2026)
    Developed novel GPCL framework with dual-level style augmentation for cross-geographic domain generalization. Achieved 36% improvement on VisDrone→IndraEye transfer (34.2%→46.5% mAP) using IoU-weighted contrastive learning.
  • Legal-Lens: AI-Powered Legal Research Platform (Aug 2024)
    Developed case prediction system using PyTorch to analyze legal data and forecast court case outcomes. Built end-to-end platform with document upload, multi-language support, and intelligent legal query resolution.
  • Multilingual Chatbot for Indian Languages (Jan-May 2025)
    Master's thesis project: Fine-tuned LLaMA-3 for real-time translation of low-resource Indian languages using AI4Bharat datasets. Implemented advanced fine-tuning strategies across multiple language pairs.
  • Semantic Image Segmentation using U-Net (March 2024)
    Designed U-Net architecture with PyTorch for pixel-level semantic segmentation. Achieved 99.48% precision, 80.15% IoU score, and 95.75% recall through advanced data augmentation techniques.
  • Neural Machine Translation Research (Jun-July 2024)
    Explored NMT models with RNN, LSTM, GRU encoder-decoder mechanisms and Transformer architectures. Implemented back translation for data augmentation in low-resource language pairs.
